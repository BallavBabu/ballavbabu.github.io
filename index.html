About

I am a quantitative economist specialising in input–output modelling and global value chain analysis. My research uses MRIO databases, structural decomposition, and value-added trade indicators to examine how Asia–Pacific production networks distribute economic gains and environmental burdens across sectors and borders.

My work focuses on the structural position of Asian economies in regional and global value chains, with particular emphasis on their bilateral production and income linkages with China and India. This approach provides sector-level evidence on whether economies move toward higher-value activities or remain concentrated in low-productivity, carbon-intensive segments of production.

I completed my Ph.D. in Economics at Yokohama National University and now continue as a Research Fellow. I welcome collaboration on empirical research related to GVC measurement, embodied emissions, bilateral income flows, and sectoral development in Asia’s evolving production systems.
