About Me

My research asks how developing economies engage with global production networks and what the distributional consequences are for their income and environment. Using rigorous multi-regional input-output (MRIO) analysis and decomposition techniques, I map the flow of value-added and embodied emissions through global value chains. This reveals which sectors and countries capture economic gains versus those that bear environmental costs.

My published work identifies the key industrial sectors driving South Asian development, while my current research builds a comprehensive framework linking bilateral income flows to structural transformation across the Asia-Pacific. Ultimately, my work provides evidence-based policy for inclusive, low-carbon industrialization by making the hidden economic and environmental trade-offs of globalization visible.

I am currently a Research Fellow at Yokohama National University, focusing on sector-level analysis of bilateral income flows in the Asia-Pacific region. I recently completed my Ph.D. in Economics from Yokohama National University in September 2025.
